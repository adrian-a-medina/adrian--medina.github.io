Introduction

This page offers a free introduction to fundamental concepts in statistics, data science, and data visualization using R for the social & behavioral sciences. Whether you're new to the field or looking to refresh your knowledge, you'll find useful explanations, examples, and practical applications of key topics. Explore the sections below to deepen your understanding of statistical methods, learn data science techniques, and visualize data effectively.

Measures of Central Tendency

This section covers the basics of mean, median, and mode, how to calculate them, and their significance in summarizing data. Learn how to implement these measures in R and interpret the results effectively.

Measures of Variability

Explore different measures of variability such as range, variance, and standard deviation. Understand how these measures provide insights into the spread and dispersion of data points around a central value, and learn to calculate them in R.

Probability

Learn the fundamental principles of probability, including conditional probability, Bayes' theorem, and the law of large numbers. This section provides R code examples to demonstrate how to compute probabilities and apply them to real-world scenarios.

Distributions

Understand different types of statistical distributions (e.g., normal, binomial, and Poisson distributions) and their applications. Use R to visualize these distributions and understand their properties.

Correlations

This section focuses on understanding the relationships between variables through correlation coefficients (Pearson, Spearman). Learn how to calculate and interpret correlations in R and visualize them using scatter plots.

Regressions

Learn about linear and multiple regression techniques, how they are used to model relationships between variables, and predict outcomes. Use R to perform regression analysis and understand diagnostics for model evaluation.

Hypothesis Testing

Discover the logic behind hypothesis testing, including setting up null and alternative hypotheses, p-values, and confidence intervals. Perform hypothesis tests in R and interpret the results.

T-Tests

This section covers one-sample, two-sample, and paired t-tests, explaining their use in comparing means between groups. Implement t-tests in R and interpret the output for decision-making.

Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)

Learn about ANOVA techniques (one-way, two-way, and repeated measures) used to analyze differences between group means. Use R to perform ANOVA tests and understand the assumptions and outputs.

Chi-Square Tests

Understand the Chi-square test for independence and goodness-of-fit, used for categorical data analysis. Apply these tests in R and learn how to interpret the results.
